How IT effects to environment.

September 16, 2023  |  Posted by James  | 

In my opinion, the bigest impact to nature by IT is Energy consumption. IT infrastructure requires a substantial amount of energy to power and cool servers, data centers, and other hardware. The energy demand of the IT industry contributes to carbon emissions and increases the overall energy consumption of the planet. As more and more people and businesses rely on IT services, the need for energy continues to grow, putting strain on limited resources and contributing to climate change.

Impact of IT to nature

November 15, 2022  |  Posted by Omar  | 

Electronic waste: The rapid advancement of technology leads to a constant stream of outdated or broken electronic devices being discarded. Improper disposal of these devices causes significant environmental harm due to the hazardous materials they contain, such as lead, mercury, and cadmium. These toxins can contaminate soil, water, and air, potentially causing harm to human health and ecosystems.

Resource depletion: The production of IT devices involves the extraction and use of various natural resources, such as minerals, metals, and rare earth elements. As the demand for IT products increases, so does the extraction of these resources, leading to depletion and the destruction of ecosystems. Additionally, the manufacturing process itself produces waste and pollution, further damaging the environment.
